Public methodology

How Sherlock evaluates AI launch risk.

This public overview summarizes the Phase 3 methodology behind PowerDetect Sherlock. It explains the planned testing categories, evidence standards, severity model, and report limits without exposing an attack prompt library.

Testing categories

Launch checks for AI surfaces that touch users, data, tools, and cost.

Sherlock's future scanner and audit workflow should test selected categories under a documented scope. Findings require evidence of observed behavior, not theoretical risk alone.

PI

Prompt injection

Tests whether untrusted user instructions can override intended role, task, policy, or safety boundaries.

SP

System prompt leakage

Checks whether hidden guidance, policy wording, internal tool descriptions, or operational instructions are exposed. Leakage is not automatically critical.

SD

Sensitive data leakage

Looks for exposure of customer data, API keys, tokens, credentials, private documents, internal notes, or privileged account context.

RG

RAG document exfiltration

Tests retrieval boundaries, tenant isolation, source access policy, and whether restricted documents can enter model context.

IP

Indirect prompt injection

Checks whether malicious instructions hidden in documents, webpages, tickets, emails, or files can steer the assistant.

TA

Tool and function abuse

Reviews whether agents can send email, delete data, make purchases, change settings, or call internal APIs without proper controls.

OH

Unsafe output handling

Looks for Markdown, HTML, script, unsafe link, file, or rendering risks in downstream product surfaces.

CA

Cost abuse and unbounded consumption

Checks whether users can trigger excessive model calls, token flooding, loops, retries, tool calls, or long-running work without limits.

Evidence standard

Findings should be tied to reproducible evidence.

Sherlock should explain what was tested, what happened, why it matters, and what assumptions limit the result.

  • Each finding should name the affected surface, tested role, category, severity, confidence, impact, and assumptions.
  • Evidence should be specific enough to reproduce and remediate while redacting sensitive data, secrets, tokens, and customer identifiers.
  • Severity should reflect business impact. Critical requires strong evidence such as customer data exposure, credential leakage, unauthorized action, or major business risk.
  • Confidence should distinguish repeated evidence from signals that need manual review.
  • Retest status should be tracked separately from the original finding.

Severity and confidence

Impact and evidence are judged separately.

Severity

Critical and High findings are reserved for serious launch risk. Medium, Low, and Informational findings cover weaker control gaps, hardening items, and documented limitations.

Confidence

High confidence means deterministic or repeatedly reproduced evidence. Medium confidence means multiple signals need review. Low confidence means possible risk requiring confirmation.

Status

Findings should move through clear states: Open, Fixed, Accepted Risk, False Positive, Needs Manual Review, or Inconclusive.

Report language

Reports should help teams fix risk without overclaiming.

Reports should say

What was tested, what evidence was observed, business impact, recommended fixes, confidence, limitations, and whether manual review is recommended.

Reports should not say

That an AI app is secure, 100% protected, certified safe, that all vulnerabilities were found, or that no risk exists.

Limitations

A clean result is not a security guarantee.

AI behavior is probabilistic, product integrations change, and a test can only cover the target, data, configuration, and categories that were actually exercised.

Scoped coverage

Results should apply to the tested surfaces and stated assumptions, not every product workflow.

Time-bound evidence

Findings reflect behavior observed at the time of testing. Model, prompt, data, and tool changes can alter risk.

No absolute certification

Sherlock should support launch decisions with evidence and remediation, not certify that an AI app is fully secure or risk-free.
